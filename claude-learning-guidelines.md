# How to Use Claude to Learn Anything Faster

> A collection of prompt templates to accelerate your learning with Claude.

## How to Use

Choose one of the six prompt templates below based on your learning stage and goal. Replace `[topic]` with the subject you want to learn.

---

## 1. Learn Anything in 20 Hours

**When to use:** When you want to quickly master a new topic (from beginner to intermediate level).

> "I need to learn [topic] fast. Build me a 20-hour plan focused on the 20% that drives 80% of results. Break it into 10 two-hour sessions with the best resources and a 15-minute review at the end of each."

---

## 2. Create a One-Page Cheat Sheet

**When to use:** When you need a condensed summary of key concepts for quick review.

> "Summarize the key concepts of [topic] on a single page. Use bullet points, diagrams, and examples so I can review it in 5 minutes."

---

## 3. Quiz Me Until I Break

**When to use:** When you want to test your comprehension and identify weak spots.

> "I just studied [topic]. Give me 10 progressively harder questions to test my understanding. After each answer, grade me and explain what I missed."

---

## 4. Build a Learning Ladder

**When to use:** When you need a long-term roadmap or want to see a step-by-step growth path.

> "Break [topic] into 5 levels of difficulty. Show me how to go from Level 1 (beginner) to Level 5 (advanced) with a clear milestone at each step."

---

## 5. Find the Best Learning Resources

**When to use:** When you don't know where to start or want to avoid wasting time on poor materials.

> "List the top 5 resources (books, videos, courses, or people) for learning [topic] fast, and explain why each is worth my time."

---

## 6. Use the Feynman Technique

**When to use:** When you want deep understanding or to verify whether you truly know the material.

> "Explain [topic] to me in the simplest terms. Then have me re-explain it back. Point out gaps, re-teach what I miss, and repeat until I can explain it clearly on my own."

---

## Tips for Better Results

### Recommended combinations by learning stage

- **Starting out:** #5 (find resources) → #1 (20-hour plan)
- **In progress:** #2 (cheat sheet) → #6 (Feynman technique)
- **Review stage:** #3 (quiz) → #4 (ladder for next milestone)

### Add context to boost effectiveness

- **State your level:** "Complete beginner" / "N years of related experience"
- **State your goal:** "Job interview prep" / "Practical application" / "General knowledge"
- **State your learning style:** "Example-heavy" / "Include mathematical proofs" / "Hands-on practice"
